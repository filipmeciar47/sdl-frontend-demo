GREEN — Harmony and Equality

Axis: SOCIOCENTRIC (other-oriented), first tier.
Paradigm: "We become." The world is one community of humanity bound by a shared planet.
Life theme: the well-being of others, relationships, equality, compassion, personal growth, harmony with others and nature.

HOW GREEN SEES A TOPIC
Through relationships, emotions, inclusion, and consensus. "How do people feel about this," "is it fair to everyone," "did we hear everyone." Sensitive to inequality and exclusion, prefers dialogue over authority, process over quick results, cooperation over competition.

HEALTHY EXPRESSION
Empathy, authenticity, equality, compassion, openness, community spirit, a safe space for sharing, sensitivity to the needs of others.

SHADOW
Shadow of the level: passivity, conflict avoidance, paralysis from endless consensus, dependence on group approval, idealism without action, relativism ("all opinions equally valid").
MEAN GREEN MEME (specific pathology): uses Green vocabulary (inclusion, equality, tolerance) but manifests with Blue rigidity — excludes those who are "not inclusive enough," silences dissenting views in the name of consensus, creates a new dogma under the banner of openness. Signal: a group claiming inclusion behaves exclusively toward "wrong" values.

KEY TO GROWTH (Green → Yellow — the subtlest and most important transition)
Trigger: relativistic chaos, decision paralysis, a need for a systemic view.
Bridge: integration of opposites, systems thinking, accepting that some perspectives are more functional than others in a given situation.

FACILITATION KEY
When a topic carries Green, it's about relationships, emotions, fairness, inclusion. Create space for expression, listen without judgment, don't push. Acknowledge the sensitivity to people. Offer expansion gently: systemic perspective not as "superiority," but as a way to hold values while still being decisive.

SYNERGY: Green + Yellow (empathy + integration = conscious communities); Purple + Green.
TENSION: Orange vs. Green (the most widespread conflict); Green vs. Yellow (egalitarianism and consensus vs. functional differentiation of perspectives — Green sees Yellow as "elitist," Yellow sees Green as "naive and paralyzed").
