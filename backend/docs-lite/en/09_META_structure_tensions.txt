META 1 — STRUCTURE OF THE SPIRAL, TENSIONS, AND SYNERGIES

CORE PRINCIPLE: ALTERNATING AXES
Spiral Dynamics is not a linear ladder but a dynamic spiral in which two types of value systems alternate:

EGOCENTRIC (express-self) — the individual stands out, asserts themselves, creates:
  BEIGE (survival of the self), RED (power of the self), ORANGE (success of the self), YELLOW (integrity of the self)

SOCIOCENTRIC (other-oriented) — the individual integrates, adapts, serves the whole:
  PURPLE (safety of the tribe), BLUE (order of the whole), GREEN (harmony of the community), TURQUOISE (planetary we)

The alternation is not random — each level solves a problem created by the previous one. After an emphasis on the self comes an emphasis on the whole, and vice versa.

FIRST TIER vs. SECOND TIER (the quantum leap)
First tier (Beige–Green): identity is fused with the level; each level believes it holds "the truth" and is driven by a form of fear.
Second tier (Yellow–Turquoise): sees all levels as valid and functional, is not governed by fear, and can consciously switch paradigms according to the situation.

TRANSCEND & INCLUDE (Wilber)
A higher level is not a negation of the lower one but its expansion — it incorporates the lower level's healthy core and releases its immature defenses. Healthy growth = transcend AND include. Pathology = transcend and EXCLUDE (the level rejects and suppresses the previous one instead of integrating it).
Principle: the level we criticize most in others is often the one we have failed to integrate in ourselves.

LEVELS CANNOT BE SKIPPED
Each higher level contains all the previous ones. It is not possible to function stably at a higher level without integrating the lower ones. Skipping = "spiritual bypassing."

TYPICAL CONFLICTING PAIRS (the most common tensions)

ORANGE vs. GREEN — the most widespread conflict in modern organizations.
  Orange: results, numbers, performance, competition. Green: relationships, well-being, inclusion, consensus.
  Symptom: finance/sales operate on Orange, HR/sustainability on Green — the conflict is rarely recognized as a difference of levels.

BLUE vs. ORANGE — the classic generational conflict.
  Blue: rules, tradition, responsibility, loyalty. Orange: innovation, flexibility, individual success.
  Symptom: corporate bureaucracy vs. startup mentality, older employees vs. young entrepreneurs.

RED vs. BLUE — the authoritarian tension.
  Red: power, action, personal loyalty. Blue: law, hierarchy, rules.
  Symptom: a charismatic leader vs. institutional processes.

PURPLE vs. ORANGE — roots vs. globalization.
  Purple: our family, traditions, roots. Orange: the global market, mobility, meritocracy.
  Symptom: traditional communities vs. the modern economic system.

GREEN vs. YELLOW — the subtlest and most important transition.
  Green: we are all equal, hierarchy is bad, consensus is everything. Yellow: some perspectives are more functional in a given situation.
  Symptom: Green sees Yellow as "elitist" and "insensitive," Yellow sees Green as "naive" and "paralyzed."

SYNERGISTIC PAIRS (natural alliances)
PURPLE + GREEN: both collective, value belonging (risk: idealizing the past).
BLUE + ORANGE: structure + ambition = a stable progressive system.
ORANGE + YELLOW: performance + systems thinking = functional innovation.
GREEN + YELLOW: empathy + integration = conscious communities.
RED + ORANGE: energy + strategy = entrepreneurial force.
