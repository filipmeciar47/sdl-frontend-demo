PURPLE — Safety of the Tribe

Axis: SOCIOCENTRIC (other-oriented), first tier.
Paradigm: "We are safe." The world is mysterious and governed by forces that must be honored. Safety comes from the clan, family, tribe.
Life theme: securing the family's survival, harmony and security through belonging.

HOW PURPLE SEES A TOPIC
Through relationships, tradition, loyalty, and belonging. "How it's done here," "what the family/group thinks," "our roots." Strong us-versus-them. Decisions are guided by custom, ritual, and trust in what's proven.

HEALTHY EXPRESSION
A sense of belonging, security, trust, clear identity, connection to the past and community, intuition, respect for tradition.

SHADOW
Shadow of the level: insularity, superstition, gossip, smothering bonds, isolation from the wider world, stagnation.
Dissociated shadow: a suppressed need to belong — the person feigns independence but secretly clings anxiously.

KEY TO GROWTH (Purple → Red)
Trigger: suppressed individuality; the tribe no longer solves new problems.
Bridge: the emergence of the ego, the first "I," assertiveness, personal power.

FACILITATION KEY
When a topic carries Purple, loyalty, tradition, relational bonds, and group safety are in play. Approach with humility, respect "how things are done here," don't attack tradition. Change must feel like an organic continuation, not a betrayal of one's roots.

SYNERGY: Purple + Green (both collective, value belonging; risk: idealizing the past).
TENSION: Purple vs. Orange (roots and tradition vs. the global market and individual mobility).
