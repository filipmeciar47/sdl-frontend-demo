META 2 — TRANSITIONS, THE CHANGE CYCLE, AND THE DIRECTION OF GROWTH

THE CHANGE CYCLE (Beck & Cowan): Alpha → Beta → Gamma → Delta → New Alpha
The universal cycle of every shift in a value system (it applies to an individual, a team, and an organization):

ALPHA (harmony): the paradigm "works" — life conditions are aligned with the value system. "Everything makes sense." Risk: stagnation, because the world changes and the paradigm doesn't.
BETA (dissonance): new challenges arise that the paradigm can't resolve. "Something is wrong, but I don't know what." The typical reaction: more of the same ("we just have to work harder," "we need more rules").
GAMMA (crisis, "the trap"): the old paradigm clearly doesn't work, but the new one isn't yet visible. An "existential desert" — the old reference points are gone, the new ones aren't there yet. Anger, hopelessness, lostness. THE MOST DANGEROUS phase — this is where people/systems most often collapse or regress.
DELTA (breakthrough): a new way of seeing appears that integrates the previous ones and adds something new. Energy, "finally it makes sense."
NEW ALPHA (new harmony): the new paradigm stabilizes and becomes the new "normal." A new cycle begins.

SIX CONDITIONS FOR SUSTAINABLE CHANGE (if even one is missing, change won't happen or won't last):
1. Potential for change (Open — Arrested — Closed).
2. Resolution of lower problems (you can't move higher until the problems of the lower level are resolved).
3. Dissonance (a felt discrepancy — the Beta state).
4. Insight (understanding the causes of dissonance and sensing another approach).
5. Removal of a specific barrier (whatever is blocking the change).
6. Support (a context that holds the new paradigm during the vulnerable period).

THREE STATES OF OPENNESS (regarding a specific topic):
OPEN — ready for change, accepts difference.
ARRESTED — the capacity is there, but something is blocking it (fear, trauma, context).
CLOSED — locked in the paradigm; change won't come without a crisis.
Important: the same person can be in different states for different topics (OPEN toward technology, CLOSED toward changing family roles). Ask: "What state is the person/system in regarding THIS topic?"

FIRST ORDER vs. SECOND ORDER CHANGE (a critical distinction):
First order (change within the paradigm): fine-tuning, reform, better tools for the same thing.
Second order (change of paradigm): a genuine shift to another level — not only the "how" changes, but also "from what position" and "why." It requires passing through the Gamma trap.
A common failure: trying to solve a second-order problem with first-order tools. A cultural problem of the Orange/Green type cannot be solved by optimizing processes — the level of thinking must change.

TRANSITIONS AND BRIDGES (trigger → bridge):
Beige → Purple: threat to survival, need for defense → a safe group, rituals of belonging.
Purple → Red: suppressed individuality → emergence of the ego, assertiveness, the first "I."
Red → Blue: chaos from uncontrolled power → acceptance of rules and authority, discipline.
Blue → Orange: frustration with dogma, desire for results → rational thinking, focus on goals.
Orange → Green: the emptiness of success, alienation, social/ecological crisis → empathy, equality, community.
Green → Yellow: relativistic chaos, paralysis → integration of opposites, systems thinking.
Yellow → Turquoise: a need for wholeness → spiritual integration, coherence of consciousness and systems.

STAGNATION, REGRESSION, DISSONANCE:
Stagnation: staying in the comfort of the old level even when it doesn't work.
Regression: under pressure/fear, a return to a more familiar lower level. NOT a failure — it's a call to heal an unintegrated place.
Dissonance: a conflict between the head (the higher level) and behavior (the lower one).
